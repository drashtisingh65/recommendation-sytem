How This Recommendation Engine Works

A quick look at the ideas powering Curatia — all running in your browser.

01

Recommendation Systems

Software that predicts what a user might like based on data — their past behavior, preferences, or the properties of items they've engaged with. You already meet these every day: "Suggested for you" rows on streaming and shopping sites.

02

Content-Based Filtering

One of the two classic approaches to recommendations (the other being collaborative filtering). It looks at the attributes of items themselves — here, genres and tags — rather than what other users liked, and recommends items with similar attributes to what you've already chosen.

03

Similarity Matching

The mathematical core of the engine. Curatia builds a "genre profile" from your selected items, then measures how much each candidate's genre set overlaps with that profile using the Jaccard similarity coefficient — shared genres divided by total unique genres involved.
